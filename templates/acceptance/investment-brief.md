# Acceptance Criteria: Investment Brief

Copy this file to `.quiver/acceptance.md` in your workspace to define what a
complete, high-quality investment brief looks like. The maker-checker will
verify each criterion before the agent writes the final deliverable.

## Structure
- [ ] Brief has company name, ticker/funding stage, and date
- [ ] Brief has a 1-paragraph investment thesis summary
- [ ] Brief has a company overview section (what they do, market)
- [ ] Brief has a financials section (revenue, growth, margins, burn rate)
- [ ] Brief has a competitive landscape section
- [ ] Brief has a risk factors section
- [ ] Brief has a valuation section (comparables, DCF, or both)
- [ ] Brief has a recommendation (buy/hold/pass with rationale)

## Financial Analysis
- [ ] Revenue figures are cited to source (earnings call, filing, press release)
- [ ] Growth rates are calculated and stated explicitly
- [ ] Margins (gross, operating, net) are stated if available
- [ ] Key metrics are benchmarked against industry peers

## Competitive Analysis
- [ ] At least 3 competitors are identified
- [ ] Competitive advantages (moats) are explicitly stated
- [ ] Market share or market position is discussed where available

## Risk Assessment
- [ ] At least 3 material risks are identified
- [ ] Regulatory risks are addressed if relevant
- [ ] Key person / concentration risks are noted if applicable

## Sources
- [ ] Every financial figure has an inline citation
- [ ] Sources include SEC filings, earnings calls, or equivalent primary sources
- [ ] No claim is made without a verifiable source
- [ ] At least 5 distinct sources are cited

## Format
- [ ] Brief is 2-5 pages (1000-2500 words)
- [ ] No placeholder text (TODO, FIXME, lorem ipsum)
- [ ] Professional tone throughout
- [ ] Clear, jargon-free language where possible
